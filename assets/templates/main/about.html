About Us

Data preparation is the initial stage in creating a machine learning model. Real-world data often lacks obvious trends and comprehensive attribute values, and it typically contains errors, partial information, and inconsistencies. Cleaning, structuring, and organizing the data is known as data preparation, and it is necessary to ensure that machine learning models can use it effectively. Begin by gathering datasets from different sources and combining them logically. Various types of datasets are accessible, including corporate datasets related to various corporations. From the gathered data, we utilize a library to extract essential information from both safe and harmful programs. We analyze hash values, API calls, debug data, major image versions, and other relevant information. This dataset, encompassing all settings and components of our system, serves as the training set. We employ 40 distinct characteristics to train the algorithm, incorporating both hazardous and safe programs during the training phase. Upon obtaining the raw data, we organize it by selecting features and assigning a source. Before altering the data, we also determine a strategy for sampling the data and then partition it into segments to create our dataset. The setup of your machine learning challenge will significantly influence the performance of these phases.
